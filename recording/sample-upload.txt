ON DECISION AND AWARENESS

The decision is made before the mind notices it. What we call choice is the
report of a choice already completed. The readiness potential rises in the dark,
and only later does the lamp of consciousness fall upon it.

If a stone became conscious while falling it would think it fell of its own free
will. The feeling of authorship is real; the authorship is not. We do not invent
the motion. We narrate it after the fact and call the narration a will.

Consciousness does not originate the act. It approves or vetoes an act already
underway. The veto is the only freedom left to it, and even the veto may be
another decision made in the dark and merely witnessed in the light.

To accept this is not to surrender. It is to model the mind as it is, rather than
as flattery would have it. The constructive move is to take the data seriously and
build on it, not to insist the data must be spurious.
