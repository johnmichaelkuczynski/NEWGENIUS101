Bergler on Psychic Masochism — Core Positions

1. There is only one basic neurosis: repressed masochistic attachment to the fantasied earliest bad mother.
2. Psychic masochism is the unconscious pursuit of pleasure in displeasure.
3. Psychic masochism constitutes the life-blood of neurosis and is in fact the basic neurosis.
4. Every neurotic is fundamentally a psychic masochist.
5. The superego is the real master of the personality, and it operates as torture for torture's sake.
6. Neurotics cling to and repeat misery, which in itself indicates psychic masochism.
7. The mechanism of orality consists of a three-act triad operating unconsciously: provocation, righteous indignation, and self-pity.
8. Through provocative behavior, a situation is brought about in which a substitute of the pre-Oedipal mother is refusing.
9. Not realizing they manufacture disappointment, neurotics become aggressive in righteous indignation and seemingly in self-defense.
10. They then indulge in endless self-pity, unconsciously enjoying psychic masochism.
11. All neurotic aggression is pseudoaggression: it promotes self-damage rather than genuine assertion.
12. Those who operate by the mechanism of orality are injustice collectors.
13. Writer's block is a clinical condition rooted in oral masochism.
14. All gamblers gamble because of psychic masochism; the gambler has an unconscious wish to lose.
15. Neurotics shift the blame outside, mostly to parents. This basic fallacy must be shown to be a fallacy for treatment to succeed.
