Ask Me Anything about Psychopathy
Freud Institute

There are those who are in this world but not of it. I am referring to psychopaths. A psychopath is someone who has no real values and therefore does not have a distinctively human psychological architecture. Most psychopaths are not criminals (even though psychopaths are disproportionately likely to go into crime). To be a psychopath is not to be criminal; it is to be a fraud. And there are many more psychopaths than most people, and even most clinicians, realize.

The term "psychopath" is ambiguous. Sometimes it refers to deeply misanthropic or anti-social people, who may have integrity, often considerable integrity. And sometimes it refers to people who utterly lack integrity. Most so-called psychopaths are psychopaths in the sense that they lack integrity. The way to test for the second kind of psychopathy is this: Are they for real? Or are their statements and actions always purely expediency-driven? Do they merely go wherever the wind is blowing? If so, they are psychopaths, in the sense that they utterly lack integrity.

Psychopathy is like cancer. And just as one can have Stage 1 or Stage 4 cancer, so one can be a Stage 1 or a Stage 4 psychopath. Psychopaths cannot benefit from treatment. Psychopathy is not so much a condition as it is a certain kind of psychological structure. Neurosis is not compatible with psychopathy, since neurosis is about a conflict between one's conscience and one's urges, and psychopaths have no conscience.
